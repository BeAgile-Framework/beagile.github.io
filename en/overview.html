Servant Leadership

In Be Agile, leaders focus on supporting the team. They work to bring security, speed and clarity to the work that needs to be done.

Agile Manager

Their mission is to create a positive environment for the team and make the workflow light, safe and optimized.

Product Designer

Their mission is to delight product audiences, understanding their expectations and designing the best solutions.

Tech Leader

Their mission is to guarantee quality and agility in solving the team's day-to-day technical tasks.

Discover and map user goals

We start by trying to better understand the audiences of the product. Who are they? What are their current experiences? How do we want to transform these experiences? From this understanding, we create a first version of the user goals map and define the delivery strategy.

1

Define the Product User Experience Proposal for each audience.

2

Discover and map user goals with your product.

3

Refine your map by studying aspects such as the importance of each user goal.

4

Build your roadmap considering a delivery strategy.

A solution design cycle for each mapped user goal

In Be Agile, the solution design is guided by the mapped user goals. This means that, at the end of a cycle, we have an user goal with its task flow well defined, prototyped and well validated. The validation with key users of the proposed solution is fundamental to give more security to the team that they are on the right path.

1

Define the task flow that the users will perform to achieve their goal

2

In addition to tasks, think now about the means by which they will be performed.

3

Define the entities, their attributes and the related rules.

4

Prototype the interfaces when applicable. Validate with key users.

5

Define the development strategy for each user goal.

A development cycle for each designed user goal

Similar to the solution design cycle, the development cycles in Be Agile are also guided by user goals. Therefore, at the end of a cycle, we deliver real value with something that can already be used. In addition, our cycles have no standardized duration and may vary according to the size of the goal.

1

Use your own UI and UX flowcharts to guide development.

2

Validate the result of each development step before proceeding.

3

Collect feedback from team members and continuously improve your process.

4

Establish an environment where people feel proud of their own work.
